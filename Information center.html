Powder coating VS Cerakoting

Both powder coating and cerakoting are popular coating options that offer superior performance and aesthetics compared to traditional paint. But there are some key differences.

Article

FAQ

Powder Coating

Powder coating is a dry finishing process that uses electrically charged dry powder to create a tough, durable coating on a variety of surfaces, typically metal. The powder is electrostatically attracted to the object and then cured with heat to form a smooth, protective layer.

The turnaround time depends on our workload and the complexity of the job. Typically, it involves pre-cleaning (1 day), coating (1-2 days), and curing (depending on the size and material). Expect a turnaround of a few days to a week.

Powder coating excels on metals like steel, aluminum, and magnesium commonly found in car parts. But it can adhere to some plastics with proper pre-treatment. Although we don't recomend powder coating plastics.

Cerakote

Cerakoting is a ceramic-based coating that offers a thin, yet extremely tough and heat-resistant finish. It's a liquid applied with a spray gun and then cured with heat. Cerakote is known for its wider range of color options and superior performance in high-heat environments.

The turnaround time depends on our workload and the complexity of the job. Generally, expect a few days for pre-cleaning, masking, coating, and curing.

Cerakote has a wider variety of colors compared to powder coating, including metallics, clears, and even custom mixes. This allows for intricate designs and personalized touches on various car parts.
